10 BEST CRYPTOCURRENCY EXCHANGES FOR TRADING CRYPTOCURRENCIES

Exchange Features
1. Binance Super easy to trade on and it's great for both beginners and advanced traders. You'll never face liquidity issues on Binance; it supports tons of coins. Binance also has no kyc under 2btc which makes things super easy when getting started. It has very low fees at just 0.05% per trade. The site is available in multiple languages such as Chinese, English, Korean, Japanese, Russian, Spanish and French. A clincher for me, as a mobile crypto trader, is their mobile app is the best cryptocurrency exchange app around (available for both iOS and Android). Once you're on the platform, there are lots of trading competitions to prove your trading powers. The sooner you get on here for trading the better.
2.Coinbase Pro Coinbase Pro is one of the early cryptocurrency exchange platforms. On Coinbase Pro, withdrawing and depositing funds are very easy. The interface is beautifully put together especially leaning towards expert traders and crypto enthusiasts. Interface tools for the power user include: candlestick charts, market data feeds and depth charts. Above and beyond this, Coinbase Pro is a compliant environment. It's insured by the New York Stock Exchange and Andresseen Horowitz. If you fancy yourself as a pro-trader, then head on over there.
3. BitMex Based out of Hong Kong, BitMex is one of the premier Bitcoin exchange platforms in the world. It provides all the tools and techniques for advanced traders as well as beginners to start trading cryptocurrencies. You can buy leveraged contracts like future or perpetual contracts, short sell and margin trade. If you want an exchange with a simple registration process and an easy to understand free structure, then BitMex is a great one to try. It's one that I use often.
4. Okex OKEx is leading digital asset exchange, providing advanced financial services to traders in many countries . OKex provides hundreds of token and futures trading pairs to help traders to optimize their strategy. Their BTC futures trade has nearly $1.5B of volume daily, and is widely recognized as a key player in the industry. OKex has a simple fee structure (spot: 0.02% - 0.2% futures: -0.01% - 0.05%). Drawbacks - OKex is not currently available for citizens of Hong Kong, Malaysia, the USA and its territories, Bangladesh and some other countries.
5. Huboi Pro Over 200 crypto pairs. Liquidity is never a problem on this exchange. They also have mobile apps for both Android and iOS for users who want to trade cryptos on the go. I found the sign up process easy to complete and their support for traders is second to none. Withdrawals and deposits are free. What's not to like!
6. Bittrex Bittrex is an US-based cryptocurrency exchange based in Seattle, Washington, where security is the most unique factor. This makes it one of the best cryptocurrency exchanges in 2019 especially for the safety of your funds. There are many cryptocurrencies listed. This one is therefore a great choice if you prioritise data and trading security. Great for US residents. Drawbacks - the exchange does have a very high turnover of cryptocurrencies leading some to accuse it of allowing pump and dump schemes.
7. Poloniex Founded by Tristan D’Agosta, Poloniex has been operational since January 2014 and is undoubtedly one of the biggest cryptocurrency exchanges in the world. It's based out of the United States and offers +100 cryptocurrencies . It's a crypto-only exchange, but you can start trading easily by depositing USDT (Tether dollars). Poloniex also has zoomable candlestick charts and a stop-limit feature (always a good safety net for all traders). Poloniex charges a fee of 0.15% to 0.25% on all trades depending upon whether you are a maker or a taker. For a crypto to crypto exchange, there's good security and decent volume and order book depth for the majority of its trading pairs.
8. CEX.IO CEX.io is one of the more popular options for buying and selling Bitcoin or any other cryptocurrency. The site offers a brokerage service that supports multiple coins and a trading platform for trading BTC, BCH, ETH and XRP. Pros -Enables buying bitcoins with credit card for low fees. One of my go-to exchanges.
9. Kraken Founded in 2011, Kraken is one of the oldest Bitcoin exchanges on the market. Over the years, its service offerings have expanded beyond just Bitcoin to include some of the world’s leading altcoins. The platform also has the distinction of being the first exchange to be listed on the Bloomberg terminals. Today, Kraken is trusted by government officials in Japan and regulated banks throughout Europe, making it one of the most respected exchanges available. Drawbacks: limited fiat currencies supported, limited payment methods, not suitable for beginners
10. Bitstamp Bitstamp is a 'Big Four' digital asset exchange that has earned the trust of the global blockchain community. It's Europe’s biggest exchange by trading volume and offers trading of BTC, ETH, LTC, BCH and XRP paired with USD, EUR, and BTC. Beginners can purchase crypto with credit cards, while experienced traders can use a range of order types and analytical tools. What's not to like!
